Ammut the Devourer

2014, Colored Pencil on Black Paper

Ammut comes from ancient Egyption lore. It has the head of a crocodile, the mane of a lion, the front of a leopard, and the haunches of a hippo - a hybrid of all the animals the Egyptians feared most. In order to gain access to the afterlife, the soul of the dead had their hearts weighed against the Feather of Truth. If the heart weighed more or less than the feather, the heart was devoured by Ammut and the deceased sentenced to eternal agony.

Art by Caitlin O'Meara, 2017.
